VidClothEditor

Efficient and Robust Video Virtual Try-On Framework via Enhanced Multi-garment Alignment

Zijian He, Peixin Chen,
SUN YAT-SEN UNIVERSITY

Abstract

Video virtual try-on aims to generate realistic sequences where garments maintain their identity and adapt accurately to a person's pose and body shape in source video. Traditional methods primarily rely on human parsing to minimize inpainting region. However, this approach limits the degrees of freedom and does not leverage the capabilities of the foundation model. In complex in-the-wild scenarios, the challenges of accurate segmentation and frame-to-frame inconsistencies in clothing agnostic regions lead to discontinuities in video try-on results. Our solution, VidClothEditor, adopts a relaxed editing approach that allows for full-body inpainting and treats unedited regions as a reconstruction task. It utilizes multiple garment alignment with region guidance to enhance the naturalness of video tryon results. Additionally, we employ garment-augmented video consistency learning, which significantly reduces the inference time and increases the practical potential for video editing. Our experiments on the VITON-HD and TikTok datasets confirm VidClothEditorâ€™s ability to generate high-quality images and smooth videos.

Framework

Overview of our VidClothEditor framework. First, we preprocess the reference video and the target upper garment by performing segmentation and pose detection. This process obtains a clothing-agnostic sequence and a pose sequence. Notice that we make full-body masking and we obtain the lower image from the first frame. Next, the target upper garment and the extracted lower garment are inputted into the GarmentNet to extract hierarchical features. Finally, the UNet incorporate aligned garments features and the conditional sequence into the denosing progress to produce video try-on results.
